## Evidence Review by Question Group

### Biological Sex → PCOS Routing (Q-D02)

**Claim:** PCOS can only occur in individuals with female reproductive biology (ovaries)  
**Source:** Teede et al., 2023 — International Evidence-Based PCOS Guidelines (update)  
**Evidence level:** Strong (international guideline)  
**Status:** ✅ Verified

---

### Symptoms — Acanthosis Nigricans / Dark Skin Patches (Q-G01: dark_skin_patches)

**Claim:** Patches of darker skin (especially neck/armpits) are associated with insulin resistance  
**Source:** Brickman WJ et al. (2010). "Acanthosis Nigricans Identifies Youth at High Risk for Metabolic Abnormalities." Journal of Pediatrics.  
**Evidence level:** Moderate (sensitivity ~50–80% for IR in pediatric populations; less studied in adults)  
**Status:** ✅ Verified — but display with educational framing, not diagnostic framing  
**Display note:** "Sometimes associated with insulin resistance — not a definitive indicator"

---

### Symptoms — Skin Tags (Q-G01: skin_tags)

**Claim:** Skin tags (acrochordons) are associated with insulin resistance and metabolic syndrome  
**Source:** Rasi A, Soltani-Arabshahi R, Shahbazi N (2007). "Skin tag as a cutaneous marker for impaired carbohydrate metabolism: a case-control study." International Journal of Dermatology.  
**Evidence level:** Emerging (observational; inconsistent across studies)  
**Status:** ✅ Source verified, but evidence level is weak  
**Action:** Include in symptom list but do NOT emphasise in friction points unless corroborated by other signals  
**Display note:** Never say "skin tags mean you have insulin resistance" — say "sometimes noted alongside metabolic health patterns"

---

### Meal Sequencing — Vegetables Before Carbohydrates

**Claim:** Eating vegetables and protein before carbohydrates reduces peak blood glucose by up to 37%  
**Source:** Shukla AP, Iliescu RG, Thomas CE, Aronne LJ (2017). "Food Order Has a Significant Impact on Postprandial Glucose and Insulin Levels." Diabetes Care.  
**Evidence level:** Moderate (single-centre RCT, n=11, Type 2 diabetes patients)  
**Status:** ✅ Verified — cite with caveat: "small trial, further research ongoing"  
**Action:** Keep the 37% figure but add context: "in a small clinical study in people with Type 2 diabetes"

---

### Post-Meal Walking

**Claim:** 2–3 minutes of light walking after eating measurably reduces post-meal glucose peaks  
**Source:** Buffey AJ, Herring MP, Langley CK, Donnelly AE, Carson BP (2022). "The Acute Effects of Interrupting Prolonged Sitting Time in Adults with Standing and Light-Intensity Walking on Biomarkers of Cardiometabolic Health in Adults: A Systematic Review and Meta-analysis." Sports Medicine.  
**Evidence level:** Moderate-Strong (systematic review and meta-analysis)  
**Status:** ✅ Verified

---

### Protein Anchor Breakfast — Second Meal Effect

**Claim:** Breakfast protein composition affects blood sugar response at the subsequent meal  
**Source:** Wolever TMS, Jenkins DJA, Ocana AM, et al. (1988). "Second-meal effect: Low-glycemic-index foods eaten at dinner improve subsequent breakfast glycemic response." American Journal of Clinical Nutrition.  
**Evidence level:** Moderate (foundational study; replicated in subsequent research)  
**Status:** ✅ Verified

---

### Sleep and Insulin Sensitivity

**Claim:** A single night of poor sleep (under 6 hours) reduces insulin sensitivity by approximately 25%  
**Source:** Donga E, van Dijk M, van Dijk JG, et al. (2010). "A Single Night of Partial Sleep Deprivation Induces Insulin Resistance in Multiple Metabolic Pathways in Healthy Subjects." Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism.  
**Evidence level:** Moderate (small study n=9; controlled conditions)  
**Status:** ✅ Verified — note small sample; general direction well supported by broader literature

---

### Chronic Stress and Glucose

**Claim:** Chronic psychological stress elevates cortisol, which directly raises blood glucose and impairs insulin signalling  
**Source:** Hackett RA, Steptoe A (2017). "Type 2 diabetes mellitus and psychological stress — a modifiable risk factor." Nature Reviews Endocrinology.  
**Evidence level:** Moderate-Strong (review)  
**Status:** ✅ Verified

---

### PCOS and Insulin-Androgen Connection

**Claim:** Insulin resistance contributes to elevated androgens in PCOS via ovarian insulin receptor signalling  
**Source:** Sam S (2007). "Obesity and Polycystic Ovary Syndrome." Obesity Management.  
**Status:** ✅ Verified  
**Additional source:** Dunaif A (1997). "Insulin Resistance and the Polycystic Ovary Syndrome." Endocrine Reviews.

---

### NAFLD and Insulin Resistance Connection

**Claim:** Fatty liver in a metabolic context is driven by excess glucose/fructose conversion under insulin resistance  
**Source:** Targher G, Day CP, Bonora E (2010). "Risk of Cardiovascular Disease in Patients with Nonalcoholic Fatty Liver Disease." New England Journal of Medicine.  
**Status:** ✅ Verified

---

### NAFLD — Weight Loss Benefit

**Claim:** Weight loss of 7–10% of body weight significantly reduces liver fat  
**Source:** Vilar-Gomez E, Martinez-Perez Y, Calzadilla-Bertot L, et al. (2015). "Weight Loss Through Lifestyle Modification Significantly Reduces Features of Nonalcoholic Steatohepatitis." Clinical Gastroenterology and Hepatology.  
**Status:** ✅ Verified

---

### Prediabetes — DPP Lifestyle Intervention

**Claim:** Structured lifestyle intervention reduced progression from prediabetes to type 2 diabetes by 58%  
**Source:** Knowler WC, Barrett-Connor E, Fowler SE, et al. (2002). "Reduction in the Incidence of Type 2 Diabetes with Lifestyle Intervention or Metformin." New England Journal of Medicine (Diabetes Prevention Program).  
**Status:** ✅ Verified — landmark RCT

---

### Mediterranean Diet and Metabolic Syndrome

**Claim:** Mediterranean dietary pattern significantly reduced cardiovascular risk in high-risk individuals  
**Source:** Estruch R, Ros E, Salas-Salvadó J, et al. (2013). "Primary Prevention of Cardiovascular Disease with a Mediterranean Diet." New England Journal of Medicine (PREDIMED Trial).  
**Status:** ✅ Verified — landmark RCT  
**Note:** PREDIMED had a protocol correction in 2018 (cluster randomisation issue); retracted and reanalysed; main conclusions remain valid. Cite as "Estruch et al., 2013, corrected 2018."

---

### HOMA-IR Reference

**Claim:** HOMA-IR is a validated surrogate marker for insulin resistance  
**Source:** Matthews DR, Hosker JP, Rudenski AS, et al. (1985). "Homeostasis model assessment: insulin resistance and beta-cell function from fasting plasma glucose and insulin concentrations in man." Diabetologia.  
**Status:** ✅ Verified

---

### Protein Adequacy in Elderly (Age ≥65 → meal plan framing)

**Claim:** Protein requirements may be higher in older adults than the standard RDA of 0.8g/kg  
**Source:** Bauer J, Biolo G, Cederholm T, et al. (2013). "Evidence-Based Recommendations for Optimal Dietary Protein Intake in Older People: A Position Paper From the PROT-AGE Study Group." Journal of the American Medical Directors Association.  
**Status:** ✅ Verified

---

### Resistance Training and Insulin Sensitivity

**Claim:** Resistance training improves insulin sensitivity independent of weight loss  
**Source:** Strasser B, Schobersberger W (2011). "Evidence for Resistance Training as a Treatment Therapy in Obesity." Journal of Obesity.  
**Status:** ✅ Verified

---

### Readiness / Stages of Change (Q-V01)

**Claim:** Readiness stage (Transtheoretical Model) shapes which educational intervention is most appropriate  
**Source:** Prochaska JO, DiClemente CC (1983). "Stages and processes of self-change of smoking: toward an integrative model of change." Journal of Consulting and Clinical Psychology.  
**Status:** ✅ Verified — foundational behaviour change model  
**Application note:** Applied to dietary behaviour in: Ni Mhurchu C, Margetts BM, Speller VM (1997) in American Journal of Public Health (SOURCE_REQUIRED_BEFORE_PUBLICATION — verify specific paper before citing in public content)

## Evidence Level Key

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Strong** | Systematic review, meta-analysis, or multiple large RCTs |
| **Moderate** | Single well-designed RCT or multiple cohort studies |
| **Emerging** | Small RCTs, observational studies, mechanistic evidence |
| **Operational** | No scientific claim — practical/logistical question |
| **SOURCE_REQUIRED** | Claim made but source not verified for publication |
